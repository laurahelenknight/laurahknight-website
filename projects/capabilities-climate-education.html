How might a capabilities approach enhance Climate Change Education in UKHE?

Upcoming Paper | 2025
Climate Change Curriculum Design Papers Upcoming

Abstract

This research examines how Amartya Sen's capabilities approach might provide a transformative framework for reimagining climate change education in UK Higher Education. Rather than focusing solely on knowledge transmission, the capabilities approach emphasizes developing students' substantive freedoms to engage meaningfully with climate challenges.

The study explores how this philosophical framework could reshape pedagogical approaches, assessment strategies, and institutional structures to foster more empowering and effective climate education.

Research Questions

  • How can the capabilities approach inform curriculum design for climate education?
  • What institutional barriers prevent students from developing climate-related capabilities?
  • How might assessment practices better reflect capabilities development rather than content mastery?
  • What role does agency play in effective climate education?

Methodology

This qualitative study employs a mixed-methods approach including:

  • Literature review of capabilities approaches in education
  • Case study analysis of existing climate education programs
  • Stakeholder interviews with educators and students
  • Participatory workshops exploring capability-centered pedagogy

Key Insights

"Education should not merely inform students about climate change, but should cultivate their capabilities to meaningfully participate in climate solutions."

Early findings suggest that traditional knowledge-focused approaches to climate education may inadvertently disempower students by emphasizing problems without fostering agency. The capabilities approach offers a framework for education that develops both understanding and empowerment.

Implications for Practice

This research has potential implications for:

  • Curriculum designers developing climate education programs
  • Institutional leaders seeking to embed sustainability across disciplines
  • Educators looking for more empowering pedagogical approaches
  • Students advocating for more meaningful climate education
